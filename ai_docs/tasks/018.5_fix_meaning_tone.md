## What Went Wrong With Task 018

Task 018 shipped three bugs:

1. **Classification label leaking** — The model writes `"direct_impact: This may affect..."` and `"indirect_signal, This shows a trend..."` as the first words of the meaning field. The instruction "classify internally" wasn't strong enough.

2. **Meaning is empty on too many stories** — The rule "if none apply, return meaning as empty string" made the model cowardly. It returned `""` for TCS revenue decline, Putin ceasefire, Israel strikes — all stories that have real meaning for Indian readers.

3. **Tone is wrong** — The meaning reads like a textbook, not like a knowledgeable friend. It says things like "may affect the outcome of future elections" instead of telling you what to actually DO or CHECK.

---

## What This Task Does

Three targeted fixes to the user prompt in `_shared.ts`:

1. **Kill the label leak** — Add explicit "Do NOT include the classification label in your output"
2. **Make meaning mandatory** — Only entertainment/animal feel-good stories get `""`. Everything else MUST have a meaning.
3. **Fix the tone** — Rewrite meaning rules to sound like a pro who lives in India explaining things to a friend. 2-3 sentences. Speak directly ("you", "your"). No vague future speculation.

---

## Context — What The User Wants

From the conversation review:

- **Tone**: Like a real person behind the screen — a pro in their own field. Not a textbook.
- **All users are Indian**: Don't say "for Indians" or "for ordinary Indians". Speak directly.
- **Government schemes**: Say who benefits concretely. "Eligible farmers will receive ₹2,000 in their accounts." NOT "If you are a farmer..."
- **No future speculation**: Say what it means NOW. Not "may affect future elections."
- **Every story gets a meaning** except entertainment and feel-good animal stories.
- **2-3 sentences** for meaning (not 1-2).

### Good vs Bad Examples

**IMF cuts growth forecast:**
> ❌ `"direct_impact: This may affect your money if you have investments or savings in countries receiving aid."`
> ✅ `"A lower global growth forecast usually weakens the rupee and can slow hiring in export-heavy sectors like IT and pharma. If you hold international mutual funds, expect short-term volatility."`

**TCS revenue decline:**
> ❌ *(empty — no green card)*
> ✅ `"TCS is India's largest IT employer. A revenue decline could mean slower hiring and tighter salary hikes across the IT sector this year."`

**Putin ceasefire:**
> ❌ *(empty)*
> ✅ `"If the ceasefire holds, global oil prices could stabilize, which directly affects petrol and diesel prices in India."`

**UP voter list released:**
> ❌ `"indirect_signal, This shows a trend connected to electoral processes in India, which may affect the outcome of future elections."`
> ✅ `"If you are a voter in Uttar Pradesh, check the final list today at your local booth or online. About 7 lakh names may be removed from the rolls."`

**Python surgery:**
> ❌/✅ *(empty — this is correct, feel-good animal story)*

  - File: `server/worldmonitor/news/v1/_shared.ts`
  - Replace lines 87–134 (the entire `userPrompt = ...` block) with:
    ```typescript
      userPrompt = `Here are news headlines about the same story from multiple sources:

${headlineText}

Write two summaries as a JSON object:

{
  "summary": "2-3 sentences. What happened, where, when, key facts.",
  "meaning": "2-3 sentences. What this means for people in India right now."
}

Rules for summary:
- 2-3 sentences max. Include actual person's name, place, and number if in the headlines.
- Never write "a politician" or "a leader" if the name is in the headlines.
- Never end with "No further details are available" or "Details are not available."
- Never add background sentences restating what the headline already says.
- NEVER invent facts not present in the headlines.

Rules for meaning:
- Write 2-3 sentences explaining what this story means for people living in India.
- Sound like a knowledgeable friend explaining the news — not a textbook or a journalist.
- Be specific and concrete: mention real effects on jobs, prices, travel, laws, markets, or daily life.
- For government schemes or policies: state who benefits and what they get. Never say "if you are a [caste/group]..." — just state the facts.
- For global events: explain the India connection (oil prices, trade, rupee, supply chains).
- For local incidents: explain what changed or what people nearby should know.
- Speak directly using "you" and "your" when appropriate.
- Do NOT speculate about the future. State what is happening or what has changed NOW.
- Do NOT include classification labels like "direct_impact:" or "indirect_signal," in your output.
- Only return meaning as empty string "" if the story is purely entertainment, celebrity gossip, or a feel-good animal story with no practical relevance.

NEVER write any of these phrases:
- "For ordinary Indians"
- "It's essential to stay informed"
- "consult a financial advisor"
- "does not directly affect most Indians"
- "highlights the importance of"
- "this may not affect"
- "may affect the outcome of future"

General rules:
- Both fields in simple English that a 16-year-old can understand
- If the story is political, remain completely neutral in both fields
- If the story is a disaster, mention relief measures if reported
- Do not start either field with "This" or "The"
- Respond ONLY with the JSON object, nothing else
`;
    ```
