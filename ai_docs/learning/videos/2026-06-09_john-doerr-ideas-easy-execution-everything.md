## TL;DR (3 bullets)

- **The title IS the lesson: "Ideas are easy, execution is everything. It takes a team to win."** Doerr's
  whole career framework reduces to this — innovation without execution is "a hallucination" (Edison).
  Startups that met all his quality criteria but still failed mostly **didn't execute** (or were too early).
- **The 5 factors that separate the Googles/Amazons from the pack:** (1) technical excellence (to attract
  A-talent), (2) outstanding founders & management, (3) strategic focus on a large/unserved market,
  (4) **execution = speed**, (5) reasonable, staged financing. The value-building move: be *ruthlessly,
  intellectually honest about your single biggest RISK* and retire it first.
- **Missionary vs Mercenary** (Komisar, *The Monk and the Riddle*): mercenaries obsess on competitors,
  sprint, chase the deal; missionaries obsess on **customers**, run a marathon, build for meaning *and*
  money. Career advice: optimize for **learning & growing** over money; pick your **field** carefully (you
  can change company easily, field rarely); aim to be a **leader**, because "ideas are easy — it's teams
  that make stuff happen."

## ELI12 — what is this actually saying?

A famous investor talks to students. His one big message, repeated all talk: **having a clever idea is the
easy part — almost worthless on its own. What actually wins is DOING it: building the thing, getting it in
front of customers, and moving fast, with a good team.** He says he's seen tons of brilliant ideas die
because nobody executed, and tons of ordinary-sounding companies win because they executed relentlessly.

His other big idea is about *why* you do the work. Some people ("mercenaries") just chase money and beat
competitors; others ("missionaries") fall in love with serving customers and a mission — and those tend to
last longer and build better things. His career advice to a 20-year-old: don't chase money first, chase
**learning**; care more about which *field* you're in than which company; and learn to **sell** and **lead
a team**, because an idea with no customers and no team goes nowhere.

## Glossary (new terms only)

- **Missionary vs mercenary company** — mercenary = opportunistic, competitor-obsessed, deal-driven, "deferred
  life plan"; missionary = customer-obsessed, mission-driven, marathon, meaning + money. *(Why it matters:
  a clean lens for SachNetra's own posture — the "be your own first customer / dataset-of-record" stance is
  a missionary framing; worth keeping it that way rather than chasing a quick mercenary monetization.)*
- **"Retire the biggest risk first"** — instead of negotiating a better deal, identify your venture's single
  largest risk (does the product work? can you land the key data partner?) and remove it up front; the
  venture is then worth more and easier to fund. *(Why it matters: maps directly onto SachNetra's "prove
  ONE edge before building a platform" sequencing — the biggest risk is "does the unique data add alpha?",
  i.e. Exp20.)*
