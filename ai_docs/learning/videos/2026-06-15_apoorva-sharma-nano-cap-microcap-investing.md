## TL;DR (3 bullets)

- **The edge is one sentence — "big money is made only when undiscovered information gets discovered" — and it
  exists only in nano/micro-caps because institutions are structurally barred.** Funds need liquidity (build a
  position in 1–3 days); a ~500-cr name trades ~₹10 lakh/day, so they can't enter until a credible 500→5000-cr
  path appears. He buys at 500, exits at ~5000 *as* MFs/FII arrive — and treats their arrival as the **exit
  bell** ("when they come, the idea is half-discounted → crowded → hard to make money"). This is the buy-side
  statement of SachNetra's own [[latency_vs_value_tradeoff]] small-cap pivot.
- **His real method is on-ground + the political-business nexus, not a data feed** (the part SachManas mostly
  *cannot* copy): factory visits (12-hr sleeper trains), promoter/auditor/customer/employee interviews, GST
  pulls, Pollution-Control-Board checks — and, crucially (Ch.15), **tracing power**: "who funds the winning
  party → who garlands Amit Shah / greets Modi at the airport → which *listed company* does that man own."
  He claims he predicted the Feb-2025 BJP-Bengal win this way. He is explicit (Ch.14): *"Claude makes the
  financial model, ChatGPT does the research — the conviction we build, no AI platform will ever be able to."*
- **Concrete, reusable signals** (Ch.15–16): **promoter buying his own shares = confidence (he weights it
  ~15%)**; promoter must have **10–15 yrs in the market** (survived a full ~3–4-yr cycle: 2021 start, 2024
  top, mid-cycle now); **land+building+cash > market cap** (replacement-value gap; his example: 210-cr MF
  holding, zero debt, 74% promoter, 160-cr cap, PE 2 vs sector 20 — and the promoter *death-threatened* him,
  he complained to SEBI). Plus behavior: **retail chases noise/upside; HNIs ask "max downside?" and size
  5–7%**; "price is God"; promoters **shake out weak hands** (10→100→20→200→80→800) and buy back cheap.

## ELI12 — what is this actually saying?

Some Indian companies are so tiny and lightly traded that the big professional investors literally *can't* buy
them — there aren't enough shares changing hands. So those companies stay invisible and almost nobody has good
information on them. This man's job is the unglamorous detective work — go to the village factory, talk to the
owner and the auditors and customers, check government tax/pollution records — to figure out *before anyone
else* that a sleepy little company is about to grow 5x–50x. He buys early and quietly, and sells exactly when
the big funds finally notice and pile in (their arrival is his sell signal).

His most striking trick is following **power**: when a party wins an election, someone paid for that campaign;
he watches who stands next to the Chief Minister, who garlands the home minister, who greets the PM at the
airport — and traces that person to the **listed company** they own, because that company is about to get
government work. He also watches the **owner's own buying**: if the boss is buying his company's shares, good
news is probably coming; if he's selling, bad news probably is. And he's honest that the *judgment* part —
the conviction — is human; "no AI can build it." (He also runs a research-subscription business, so treat his
return stories as advertising, not proof.)

## Glossary (new terms only)

> Checked against `wiki/glossary.md` — none are in it yet.

- **Nano-cap (vs penny stock)** — a *very* small listed company (tens to a few hundred cr) with a **real,
  growing business** that's undiscovered. A penny stock has *no real business*.
- **Patron-link / political-business nexus** — a connection between a politician/party and a businessman who
  funds them; the businessman's *listed* company then benefits from government work. (= the `patron-link` edge
  in the SachManas spec; Fisman 2001: political connections ≈ up to 25% of connected-firm value.)
- **Promoter buyback signal** — promoter buying his *own* company's shares from the market = he expects good
  news (and vice-versa for selling). Apoorva weights it ~15%.
- **Replacement-value gap** — when land + building + cash (net of debt) exceeds market cap, i.e. the company
  is worth more dead than the market prices it alive; a value-entry trigger.
- **Weak-hand shakeout** — deliberate price volatility that scares uninformed/short-term holders out so the
  promoter (or informed buyers) can accumulate cheap.
- **"Price is God"** — (attrib. Harshad Mehta) price is the final arbiter; when the thesis and the tape
  diverge persistently, the tape wins (→ stop-loss).
- **Pre-IPO / open offer / Muhurat report** — (defined in the earlier draft; unchanged.)

## State of the market RIGHT NOW (per this source)

Two durable claims (both structural, not timing):

1. **Asymmetry claim** — information asymmetry is arbitraged out of large-caps but **persists in micro/nano-caps**
   because institutional liquidity constraints physically bar funds below ~₹500 cr.
   - **If true** → the only edge left for a slow, public-data participant is small/micro-cap events institutions
     can't pre-empt — exactly SachNetra's chosen escape ([[latency_vs_value_tradeoff]], [[project_pead_midcap_finding]]).
   - **Falsifiable by** evidence that small-cap event returns aren't larger / less efficiently priced once you
     control for liquidity, size, survivorship — what the mid-cap PEAD work tests.
2. **Cycle claim** — Indian equities run a **~3–4-yr cycle** (2021 start → 2024 top → mid-cycle now, an
   up-leg "in the next 1–2 years"). Time horizon: cycle. *(Unfalsifiable as stated; a cheap prior, not a signal.)*

Everything else (Indri, the unnamed 20–25x, "7x in 3–6 months", pre-IPO 3–4x, the Bengal/Odisha calls) is
**un-verifiable, survivorship-selected anecdote from someone selling research products** — motivation, not
evidence.
