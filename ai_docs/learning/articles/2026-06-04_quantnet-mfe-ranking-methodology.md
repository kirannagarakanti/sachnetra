# QuantNet Ranking of Best Financial Engineering Programs (2025)

> **Why Lijo read this**: Which MFE programs actually place students into quant roles, and how is "best" measured?

---

## TL;DR (3 bullets)

- QuantNet ranks MFE/MathFin programs yearly using **employment rate (3 months post-grad)**, **average starting salary + sign-on bonus**, and **employer feedback** — not brand prestige.
- Princeton Master in Finance topped the 2025 list; the ranking rewards programs with verifiable, granular placement data.
- This is the most data-driven MFE ranking in the industry — it is peer-reviewed by the firms hiring from these programs, making it more signal-rich than US News–style rankings.

---

## ELI12 — what is this actually saying?

Every year QuantNet asks universities: "How many of your graduates got jobs in finance within 3 months? What was their first-year pay?" Then it asks the companies hiring them: "Is this program sending us good candidates?" It mashes those numbers together and publishes a league table. It is like a school report card, but the grade comes from employers, not examiners. Programs that hide their placement data or have bad outcomes drop.

## Glossary (new terms only)

- **MFE (Master of Financial Engineering)** — A postgraduate degree (typically 1 year) focused on math, programming, and finance for quantitative roles. Variants: MMF (Mathematical Finance), MSF (Mathematical Statistics for Finance), MQF.
- **Sign-on bonus** — A one-time cash payment at the start of employment, separate from annual salary. Standard in quant roles, often $20k–$100k.
- **Cohort size** — Number of students enrolled in a program year; smaller cohorts = more selective.
- **Employer feedback score** — Anonymous ratings from hiring firms on graduate quality; acts as a "client review" for the program.
