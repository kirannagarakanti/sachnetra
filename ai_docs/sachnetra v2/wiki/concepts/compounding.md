# Compounding

**TL;DR:** Compounding is earning returns on your returns — money, data, and trust all compound; the edge is starting early and staying consistent.

## Core Mechanics

- ₹100 at 10%/year → ₹110 after year 1 → ₹121 after year 2 (not ₹120)
- The difference (₹1) is the compound effect — small at first, massive over decades
- Formula: `A = P × (1 + r)^n` where P = principal, r = rate, n = years
- Rule of 72: divide 72 by annual rate = approximate years to double (72 / 10% = 7.2 years)

## Why It Beats Linear Growth

- Linear: ₹100 × 10% × 10 years = ₹200
- Compound: ₹100 × (1.10)^10 = ₹259 — 30% more with zero extra effort
- At 20 years: linear = ₹300, compound = ₹673 — the gap accelerates

## Indian Market Context

- Nifty 50 long-run return: ~12%/year CAGR
- At 12%: money doubles every 6 years
- SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) forces compounding by automating periodic investment

## The SachNetra Parallel

- Data compounds: 30 days of sentiment data → weak signal; 12 months → sellable dataset
- Every article stored today cannot be recovered retroactively — lost days = lost compounding
- Source credibility scores compound from feedback: 1,000 ratings → weak signal; 10,000 → training data
- User trust compounds: early adopters become contributors who improve the product

## Key Insight

"The library vs scattered books" — organised, consistent data compounds in value; scattered data does not.
Quantchics confirmed: sentiment signals need 90-day smoothing to remove noise — compounding the signal through time makes it actionable.
