# Academic Research Ingestion & Preprint Latency

**TL;DR:** Minimizing alpha decay by systematically tracking, parsing, and backtesting quantitative trading models from preprint networks (SSRN/arXiv) before they are formally published.

## 1. Preprint Latency Edge
- **Publication Lag:** The peer-review process typically takes 12–24 months. By the time a strategy is published, its alpha has often decayed due to market awareness. Ingesting preprints preserves the edge.
- **Microstructure & Macro Updates:** Preprints capture immediate responses to market reforms, regulatory changes, or newly observed anomalies.

## 2. SSRN eLibrary Taxonomy
- **Research Networks:** Broad discipline-specific archives.
  - **Financial Economics Network (FEN):** Main network for asset pricing, derivatives, and market microstructure.
  - **Economics Research Network (ERN):** Focuses on monetary cycles, emerging market macroeconomics, and central bank (RBI) policy.
  - **Accounting Research Network (ARN):** Financial disclosures, PEAD, and earnings news.
  - **Corporate Governance Network (CGN):** Promoter pledging and auditor changes.
- **eJournals:** Curated email abstracting newsletters that distribute papers on specific sub-topics (e.g., *Capital Markets: Market Microstructure eJournal*).
