# Inflation

**TL;DR:** Inflation erodes purchasing power — India's 8.2% food inflation (April 2026) is what blocked RBI from cutting rates despite easing core inflation.

## Measures

- **CPI** (Consumer Price Index): tracks price of basket of goods for households — the headline number
- **WPI** (Wholesale Price Index): tracks prices at wholesale level — leading indicator
- **Food inflation**: India's most volatile component — monsoon-driven, politically sensitive
- **Core inflation**: CPI excluding food and fuel — more stable, what RBI watches for policy

## India-Specific Dynamics

- Food = ~40% weight in India's CPI (vs ~10% in US)
- Monsoon season is the biggest inflation risk in India — bad monsoon → food prices spike
- Agricultural data is a leading indicator for Indian inflation: kharif crop + rabi crop seasons
- SachNetra has opportunity: agri news in Hindi/regional languages breaks before English media

## RBI Reaction Function

```
Food inflation high → Core inflation stable → RBI holds rates
Both falling      → RBI signals possible cut in Q3
Both high         → RBI raises rates
```

April 2026 example: 8.2% food inflation kept rates at 6.25% despite market hoping for a cut.

## Inflation's Effect on Assets

| Asset Class | High Inflation | Low Inflation |
|---|---|---|
| Bonds | Bad (real yield falls) | Good (real yield rises) |
| Equities | Mixed (pricing power varies) | Good (margins expand) |
| Gold | Good (store of value) | Neutral |
| Real Estate | Good (hard asset) | Neutral |
