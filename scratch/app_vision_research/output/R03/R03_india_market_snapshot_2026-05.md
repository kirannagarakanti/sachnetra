### 3. Advance/Decline Habit (Typical Daily Breadth)
- **Equilibrium Breadth:** On a typical neutral day on the National Stock Exchange (NSE), which hosts ~2,000 listed equities, the Advance-Decline (A/D) ratio clusters around **1.0** (~1,000 advancing vs. ~1,000 declining stocks).
- **Strongly Bullish Sessions:** Readings **above 2.0** denote strong, broad-based retail and institutional participation (e.g. 1,400 advances vs. 600 declines).
- **Bearish/Panic Sessions:** Readings **below 0.5** indicate systematic risk-off waves (e.g. 500 advances vs. 1,500 declines).

## Part D — Market Structure Rules

### 1. Settlement Cycle
- **T+1 settlement:** Fully implemented. Equities settled on $T+1$ cycle. Instantaneous settlement ($T+0$) is undergoing pilot trials for select liquid securities.

### 2. Margins & Intraday Leverage
- **Upfront Margin Collection:** Mandated 100% upfront collection of option premiums from buyers to prevent excessive leveraging.
- **Intraday Positions:** Monitored dynamically (minimum of 4 random snapshots taken by clearing corporations daily) to enforce client-level limits in real-time.

### 3. Surveillance Measures
- **ASM/GSM Lists:** Stocks exhibiting extreme volatility or lack of financial fundamentals are routed to the Additional Surveillance Measure (ASM) or Graded Surveillance Measure (GSM) segments.
- **Enhanced Surveillance Measure (ESM):** Implemented specifically to track micro-cap and small-cap stocks, narrowing price bands to 2% or 5% and requiring trade-to-trade settlement.

### 4. Deal Disclosures (V2-030 Ingestion Schema)
- **Bulk Deals:** Triggered when the total quantity traded by a single client exceeds **0.5%** of the company's listed equity. Must be disclosed on EOD basis.
- **Block Deals:** Single large transactions with a minimum notional value of **₹10 crore** executed during dedicated 15-minute trading windows (morning and afternoon).
  - *Source & Deep Link:* [zerodha.com](https://zerodha.com/varsity/chapter/corporate-disclosures-bulk-and-block-deals/) · `confirmed`

## Part F — "Temperature Check" Synthesis

### 1. Regime Label: **Large-cap correcting; mid-caps & cyclicals resilient**
- **Fact 1:** The Nifty 50 has declined **-8.56% YTD** (as of May 27, 2026) and remains **-9.21%** below its Jan 2, 2026 All-Time High of 26,331.40.
- **Fact 2:** Foreign Institutional Investors (FPIs) have been aggressive net sellers, withdrawing a cumulative **-₹34,468.89 crore** from Indian equities MTD (as of May 27, 2026).
- **Fact 3:** Higher transaction taxes (STT hike effective April 1, 2026) and strict SEBI F&O weekly options restrictions have dampened overall speculative trading volumes.

### 2. Retail Mood (Pointers to R04/R06)
- Retail mood is shifting from high-leveraged day trading to structured mutual fund allocations. This is validated by strong monthly SIP flows (**₹31,115 crore** in April 2026) absorbing index volatility, although speculative interest remains pockets-only (e.g. SME IPO listings).

### 3. Institutional Mood
- **Foreign (FIIs):** Strongly risk-off. Heavy equity selling driven by high US Treasury yields, USD strength, and currency depreciation pressures (USD/INR near 95.8).
- **Domestic (DIIs):** Steadfastly risk-on. Actively buying to absorb FII outflows, recording a cumulative **+₹63,445.19 crore** MTD net buy flows (as of May 27, 2026) supported by domestic retail liquidity.

### 4. Biggest 3 Risks (Next 90 Days)
1. **West Asian Geopolitical Shocks:** Escalation risks impacting crude oil import prices and maritime trade routes.
2. **Persistent Dollar Strength & Yields:** Continued high US interest rates pushing USD/INR past 96 and accelerating FII equity flight.
3. **WPI Inflation Pass-through:** The sharp rise in WPI (8.30% in April 2026) could pass through to retail CPI, forcing the RBI to reverse its neutral stance.

### 5. Biggest 3 Tailwinds
1. **Resilient Domestic Inflow Ingestion:** Steady retail SIP contributions (~₹31k crore/month) and positive DII equity mutual fund net inflows (~₹38k crore/month).
2. **Aggressive Capex Allocations:** Fiscal push via **₹12.2 lakh crore** capex (11.5% YoY growth) supporting capital goods, infrastructure, and defense sectors.
3. **Attractive REER Valuations:** The REER dropping to a 13-year low of **90.96** makes Indian exports highly competitive in international markets.
