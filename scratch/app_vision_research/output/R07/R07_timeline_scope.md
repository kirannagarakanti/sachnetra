## 3. Appendix: Nov 2016 Demonetization Comparator

*Note: Provided as a historical structural comparator to distinguish liquidity shocks from external geopolitical/policy shocks.*

### Overview
On **November 8, 2016**, at 20:00 IST, Prime Minister Narendra Modi announced that all ₹500 and ₹1,000 banknotes (constituting **86%** of currency in circulation) would cease to be legal tender effective midnight. The primary goal was targeting black money and promoting digitization.

### Market Response & Timeline
1.  **Immediate Impact (9-Nov-2016):**
    *   **Nifty 50:** Opened down over 6% (also impacted by Donald Trump winning the US Presidential Election that same morning), but recovered to close down **1.31%** (settling at 8,432.00).
    *   **USD/INR:** Depreciated by 0.3% to close at 66.43.
2.  **30-Day Liquidity Contraction:**
    *   Nifty fell to a low of **7,929.10** on November 21, 2016 (a peak-to-trough decline of **7.5%** post-announcement).
    *   FPIs withdrew **-₹18,244 crore** in November 2016, driven by EM risk-off from Trump's victory and local cash crunch uncertainty.
3.  **Sector Impact:**
    *   **Laggards (Cash-dependent):** Nifty Realty fell **-18%** in the month post-announcement. Automobiles, jewelry, and microfinance experienced sharp drops due to cash transaction paralysis.
    *   **Resilient / Outperformers:** Metals and IT services (less cash-reliant, export-driven). NBFCs and banks saw high short-term deposits, though lending stalled.

### Key Divergences from Modern Shocks (2020–2026)
*   **Liquidity vs. Valuation:** Demonetization was a domestic transactional liquidity shock. The banking system was flooded with cash deposits, lowering cost of funds, whereas modern shocks (like US Fed hikes or 2026 FPI flights) saw systemic dollar drainage and capital flight.
*   **SIP Cushion Absence:** In 2016, retail SIP inflows were less than ₹3,500 crore/month (vs. **₹31,115 crore/month** in April 2026). DIIs lacked the massive domestic liquidity cushion they have today, meaning FPI outflows had a larger direct impact on index levels.
