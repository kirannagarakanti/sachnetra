# R07 — Narrative Archaeology: Persisted vs. Fad

*Author: Antigravity · Status: Active · As-of: 27-May-2026*

This document audits the evolution of market narrative during major geopolitical and domestic shocks since 2020, evaluating whether these narratives represented long-term structural shifts (**persisted**) or short-term noise (**fad**) that the price eventually corrected.

## 2. Archaeological Deep Dives

### Case 1: The Adani Hindenburg Narrative (Jan–Feb 2023)
*   **Media Coverage (ET/Mint/BS):** "Adani stock rout wipes out $120B in market value; SEBI probes offshore entities; SBI exposure under scanner."
*   **Social Sentiment (Telegram/Reddit):** Channels like `@desiwallstreet` and Reddit's `r/IndiaInvestments` were filled with claims of "Lehman-like moment for Indian banks."
*   **The Disconnect:** The narrative treated the crisis as a banking insolvency risk. In reality, the Adani Group companies held productive, cash-generating assets (ports, transmission lines, airports) which continued operations unimpeded. The debt was refinanced, and GQG's $1.87 billion investment in March 2023 completely broke the short-seller momentum.
*   **Key Lesson:** Media narratives often conflate stock price drops with asset insolvency. Systemic risks are low when underlying assets generate utility cash flows.

### Case 2: Election Results Day (June 4, 2024)
*   **Media Coverage (CNBC-TV18/Moneycontrol):** "Worst day for Indian markets in 4 years; PSU banks and defense stocks lock in lower circuits; policy stagnation fears."
*   **Social Sentiment (Telegram/Reddit):** Heavy panic, retail posting "Modi era over, buy FMCG and sell all PSUs."
*   **The Disconnect:** The narrative assumed that a coalition government would immediately stop capital expenditure and return to populist spending. However, the FY25 and FY26 budgets demonstrated that the government continued its capex-heavy infrastructure model (raising capex to **₹12.2 lakh crore** in early 2026). The market recovered its entire June 4 losses within three trading sessions.
*   **Key Lesson:** Retail sentiment overreacts to headline political outcomes, failing to model the long-term momentum of state fiscal policies.

### Case 3: West Asia Escalation & FPI Outflows (May 2026)
*   **Media Coverage (ET/Financial Express):** "Rupee hits fresh lows past 95.80; FII flight continues as Brent crude hovers near $110; WPI inflation spikes to 8.30%."
*   **Social Sentiment (Telegram/Reddit):** High levels of frustration regarding SEBI's weekly options restrictions and STT hikes, but overall structural confidence in mutual fund SIP allocations.
*   **The Disconnect:** The global narrative is heavily risk-off, expecting a major currency crisis in India. However, the domestic narrative (as observed in R06 Telegram themes) is focused on rotating money from underperforming IT (~-23.8% YTD) and Auto into Metals (+19% YTD) and Energy (+17% YTD). The ₹31k crore monthly SIP flow provides a permanent structural bid that prevents a panic-induced cascade.
*   **Key Lesson:** The "FII selling = market crash" narrative is historically dead. Domestic structural inflows (DIIs) have decoupled index levels from FPI capital flight, converting systemic collapses into sector rotations.
